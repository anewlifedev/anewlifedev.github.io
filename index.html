ABOUT THE COMPANY

A New Life is a registered 501(c)(3) charity working to provide prosthetics to children in developing nations

Why we do it

Half of our founding members come from developing nations, and have witnessed first hand the poor prospects that face many amputees in those places. But all of us share a passion to improve the world, and we realized that we could have a huge impact in the lifes of thousands of people across the planet by simply providing them with prosthetics. Such a simple thing can completely transform someones life.

Why do we focus on children?

We decided to focus on children because we saw that all of the challenges faced by amputees were multiplied in the case of children. With reduced mobility, many will miss out on recieving an education if they are not capable of walking to school, they may not be able to get a job, may never marry, and place a burden on family and community. This issues are all compunded with the fact that children will quickly outgrow their prosthetics, requiring replacements every 2 - 3 years.

How does our system work?

We have created a network by connecting workers on the ground of these countries who can identify the children in need of prosthetics, and get measurements for them, with the design and manufacturing resources around the world. By using the internet as our platform for delegating work, we can create a decentralized design group composed students and professionals worldwide. This decentralized group can design the prosthetics needed, while never having to leave the comfort of their own home, gaining valuable experience all the while. Then all that's left is to use our manufacturing partners to assemble the final product and ship it to our processing facility so it can be delivered to the child in need.

How to get involved

Are you ready to make a difference in a childs life? All you need to do is register on our website, and select what type of role you want to be in: financial sponser, manufacturing sponser, or designer. As a financial sponser you simply provide us with the funds we need to operate and reach as many children as possible, all of this money is reported transparently and tax deductable.

As a manufacturing sponser you are agreeing to provide us with a certain amount of manufactured parts per year, exactly how many is completely up to you.

As a designer you are entering into our system to actually design the prosthetics that will be delivered. Often these design are as simple as modifying an existing design to be an exact fit, or making slight modifications, but the designs can get as complex as you are capable of, so long as it is practical. To enter into a design role you must prove that you have a background in engineering, biomedical, or general design and production. After you are appproved, you can set out designing prosthetics for children all over the world.
